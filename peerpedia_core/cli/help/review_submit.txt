Submit a peer review of someone else's paper.  Your review includes
numerical scores and a written comment.  Reviews are public and signed
with your key — they become part of the paper's permanent record.

SCORING DIMENSIONS (each 1–5, from poor to exceptional)
  orig     originality    — novelty of the idea or approach
  rigor    rigor          — methodological soundness
  comp     completeness   — coverage of edge cases and limitations
  ped      pedagogy       — clarity of exposition
  imp      impact         — potential significance to the field

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       → you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       → you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       → or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --title means "the title comes next"
                       --scores means "the scores come next"
                       Some flags take a value (--title "My Paper")
                       Others work alone (--mine, --force, --feed).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.


EXAMPLES
  # Review a paper by its ID
  peerpedia review submit abc12345 \
      --scores "orig=4,rigor=3,comp=4,ped=3,imp=5" \
      --comment "This paper presents a compelling argument for..."
  # → Scores are recorded and your comment becomes part of the permanent
  #   review record for this paper.

  # Review a paper by its title (PeerPedia finds the best match)
  peerpedia review submit "Radioactivity" \
      --scores "orig=5,rigor=4,comp=4,ped=5,imp=5" \
      --comment "An excellent treatment of the subject..."
  # → PeerPedia finds the closest title match and submits your review.

  # What happens if your comment is too short
  peerpedia review submit abc12345 \
      --scores "orig=4,rigor=3,comp=4,ped=3,imp=5" \
      --comment "Good paper"
  # → Error: comment must be at least 200 characters (yours was 11).
  #   PeerPedia requires thoughtful reviews that help authors improve.
  #   Write at least a few sentences explaining your scores.

  # Understanding the output — what a successful review shows
  peerpedia review submit abc12345 \
      --scores "orig=4,rigor=3,comp=4,ped=3,imp=5" \
      --comment "This paper makes a strong contribution to the field..."
  # → Your scores are shown back to you as a confirmation table:
  #   Originality:   4/5   Rigor:   3/5   Completeness:  4/5
  #   Pedagogy:      3/5   Impact:  5/5
  #   Your review is now public and signed with your key.

WHAT MAKES A GOOD REVIEW
  • Be specific — point to sections, arguments, or data.
  • Explain your scores — why did you give a 3 instead of a 4?
  • Suggest improvements, not just criticism.
  • Your review is signed and public — write professionally.

TIPS
  • The 200-character minimum is about substance, not word count.
    Aim for 3-5 sentences: what the paper does well, what could improve,
    and why you gave the scores you did.
  • You cannot edit or delete a review once submitted. Re-read your
    comment before hitting enter.
  • Use the paper's ID (abc12345) for exact targeting. Title matching
    works if the title is unique enough.

FLAGS
  Each flag is shown in the examples above.  This reference explains them
  in full — come back after you have tried the examples.

  --scores "dim=val,dim=val,..."
                       Your five numerical scores, one for each scoring
                       dimension above.  Each score is 1 (poor) through
                       5 (exceptional).  Format: "orig=4,rigor=3,comp=4,
                       ped=3,imp=5".  Use short names: orig (originality),
                       rigor (rigor), comp (completeness), ped (pedagogy),
                       imp (impact).  All five dimensions are required.

  --comment "text"     Your written review (required, at least 200
                       characters).  Explain what the paper does well,
                       what could be improved, and why you gave each
                       score.  Use quotes around your comment if it has
                       spaces or special characters.

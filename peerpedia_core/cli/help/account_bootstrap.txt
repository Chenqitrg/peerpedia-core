Set up the same PeerPedia identity on a new device.  This creates a
minimal user record on the new machine so you can recover your signing
key with your password.

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       -> you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       -> you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       -> or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --from means "the JSON blob comes next"
                       --peer means "the server URL comes next"
                       Some flags take a value (--from '{"json"}')
                       Others work alone (--json, --rich).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.

EXAMPLES
  # Step 1 — On your original device, export your identity as JSON.
  peerpedia account whoami --verbose --json
  # -> {"user_id": "abc12345-...", "name": "Marie Curie",
  #     "public_key": "...", "salt": "..."}

  # Step 2 — On your new device, paste the JSON from step 1.
  # This creates a minimal record so PeerPedia knows who you are.
  peerpedia account bootstrap --from '{"user_id":"abc12345-...","name":"Marie Curie","public_key":"...","salt":"..."}'

  # Step 3 — Verify your password and load your session on the new device.
  # PeerPedia re-derives your signing key and saves the session.
  peerpedia account recover --user-id abc12345

  # Bootstrap and immediately connect to a peer server for data sync.
  # Use this when you want to download your papers right away.
  peerpedia account bootstrap --from '<json>' --peer https://peer.example.com

  # Bootstrap with machine-readable confirmation (for scripts).
  peerpedia account bootstrap --from '<json>' --json
  # -> {"id": "abc12345-...", "name": "Marie Curie"}

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER BOOTSTRAP
  • Your new device has a user stub — enough for PeerPedia to know who
    you are and verify your password.
  • You still need to run "account recover" (step 3) to actually log in.
  • Your papers are NOT transferred yet.  Run "sync pull --server <url>"
    to download your articles from a peer server.
  • If you used --peer, PeerPedia also syncs your social graph (who you
    follow and who follows you).

WHEN TO USE BOOTSTRAP vs REGISTER
  • Use "bootstrap" when you already have an account and want to use it
    on a second device (laptop, lab computer, etc.).
  • Use "register" when you are creating a brand-new identity for the
    first time.

TIPS
  • The JSON blob contains your public key and salt — this is public
    information.  It does NOT contain your password or private key.
  • Copy the JSON from your original device over SSH, a USB drive, a
    QR code, or any method you trust.
  • After bootstrap + recover, run "sync pull" to get your articles and
    "sync discover" to find peers in your network.

FLAGS
  --from      The JSON blob containing your identity, as printed by
              "account whoami --verbose --json" on your original device.
              Required.  Shown in the "Step 2" example.
  --peer      A peer server URL for data sync right after bootstrap.
              Use this to download your articles without a separate
              "sync pull" step.  Shown in the "Bootstrap with peer" example.
  --json      Print the result as JSON instead of human-friendly text.
              Default: off.  Shown in the "Bootstrap with --json" example.
  --rich      Print the result as formatted Rich text (the default).

SEE ALSO
  account whoami --verbose    Export your identity from your original device
  account recover             Verify your password after bootstrap
  sync pull                   Download your papers to the new device
  sync discover               Find peers in your network

Submit your paper to the sedimentation pool for public peer review.
Once published, other researchers can find and review your work.
After a 3-day review period, the paper auto-publishes.

EXAMPLES
  # Publish with self-review scores
  peerpedia article publish abc12345 \
      --scores "orig=4,rigor=3,comp=4,ped=3,imp=3"

  # Publish by title keyword (if you don't remember the ID)
  peerpedia article publish "Radioactivity" \
      --scores "orig=5,rigor=4,comp=3,ped=4,imp=5"

SCORING YOUR OWN PAPER (each dimension 1–5)
  orig    originality    — How novel is your idea?
  rigor   rigor          — How sound is your methodology?
  comp    completeness   — How thorough is your coverage?
  ped     pedagogy       — How clear is your writing?
  imp     impact         — How much potential influence?

  Example scores and what they mean:
    "orig=2,rigor=5,comp=5,ped=4,imp=3"  → thorough survey, not very novel
    "orig=5,rigor=4,comp=4,ped=5,imp=5"  → groundbreaking, well-written
    "orig=3,rigor=3,comp=3,ped=3,imp=3"  → average across the board

WHAT HAPPENS NEXT
  1. Your paper enters the sedimentation pool (public but not yet "published").
  2. Other users can find and review it during this period.
  3. After 3 days, it auto-publishes — or run "article scan" to speed it up.
  4. Published papers are permanent and citable.

PREREQUISITES
  • You must be logged in and be a maintainer of the paper.
  • If there are co-authors, ALL must consent first (see maintainer consent).

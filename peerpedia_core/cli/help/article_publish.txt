Submit your paper to the sedimentation pool for public peer review.
Once published, other researchers can find and review your work.
After a 3-day review period, the paper auto-publishes.

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       → you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       → you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       → or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --title means "the title comes next"
                       --scores means "the scores come next"
                       Some flags take a value (--title "My Paper")
                       Others work alone (--mine, --force, --feed).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.


EXAMPLES
  # Publish with self-review scores
  peerpedia article publish abc12345 \
      --scores "orig=4,rigor=3,comp=4,ped=3,imp=3"

  # Publish by title keyword (if you don't remember the ID)
  peerpedia article publish "Radioactivity" \
      --scores "orig=5,rigor=4,comp=3,ped=4,imp=5"

  # Check that your paper entered the pool
  peerpedia article publish abc12345 \
      --scores "orig=4,rigor=3,comp=4,ped=3,imp=3"
  # → Paper enters sedimentation.  Verify with:
  #     peerpedia article list --status sedimentation

  # What happens if you forget --scores
  peerpedia article publish abc12345
  # → PeerPedia refuses and shows an error.
  #   You must score your own paper in all 5 dimensions before publishing.

SCORING YOUR OWN PAPER (each dimension 1–5)
  orig    originality    — How novel is your idea?
  rigor   rigor          — How sound is your methodology?
  comp    completeness   — How thorough is your coverage?
  ped     pedagogy       — How clear is your writing?
  imp     impact         — How much potential influence?

  Example scores and what they mean:
    "orig=2,rigor=5,comp=5,ped=4,imp=3"  → thorough survey, not very novel
    "orig=5,rigor=4,comp=4,ped=5,imp=5"  → groundbreaking, well-written
    "orig=3,rigor=3,comp=3,ped=3,imp=3"  → average across the board

  These are the same 5 dimensions used when reviewing others' papers.
  See "review submit --help" for a detailed explanation of each.

WHAT HAPPENS NEXT
  1. Your paper enters the sedimentation pool (public but not yet "published").
  2. Other users can find and review it during this period.
  3. After 3 days, it auto-publishes — or run "article scan" to speed it up.
  4. Published papers are permanent and citable.

PREREQUISITES
  • You must be logged in and be a maintainer of the paper.
  • If there are co-authors, ALL must consent first (see maintainer consent).

FLAGS
  Each flag is shown in an example above.  This is the full reference.

  --scores "dim=val"  Your self-review scores for the paper.
                      Required.  All 5 dimensions, each 1–5:
                        orig  = originality
                        rigor = rigor
                        comp  = completeness
                        ped   = pedagogy
                        imp   = impact
                      Format: "orig=4,rigor=3,comp=4,ped=3,imp=3"
                      Example: shown in "Publish with self-review scores"
                      above.

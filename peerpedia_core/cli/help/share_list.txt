View your share feed or the articles you've shared.  Shares are
recommendations — when you share a paper, your followers see it.

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       → you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       → you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       → or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --title means "the title comes next"
                       --scores means "the scores come next"
                       Some flags take a value (--title "My Paper")
                       Others work alone (--mine, --force, --feed).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.


EXAMPLES
  # See what others have shared (your feed)
  peerpedia share list
  # → Shows recent shares from people you follow.  Each entry shows
  #    the article title and who shared it.

  # See your own shares
  peerpedia share list --mine
  # → Shows articles you've shared, with any comments you added.
  #    Use this to review what recommendations you've made.

  # View your feed to discover new reading
  peerpedia share list
  # → Your feed is a stream of recommendations from people you follow.
  #    No shares yet?  Follow more people with:  peerpedia follow @name

  # Check if a particular share went through
  peerpedia share list --mine
  # → Scan the output.  If you don't see the article you shared, it may
  #    not have been posted.  Try:  peerpedia share add <id>

WHAT SHARE ENTRIES LOOK LIKE
  • In your feed (no --mine): shows the article title and ID.
    Use "peerpedia article show <id>" to read the paper.
  • In your own shares (--mine): shows the article ID and any comment
    you included.  Useful for remembering why you shared something.

FLAGS
  --mine    Show only the articles YOU have shared, instead of your
            feed.  Use this to review or manage your own shares.
            Shown in example 2 above.

  --json    Output the list as JSON instead of the table format.
            Useful for scripts and automation.

  --rich    Force human-readable colored output (default when
            you type the command directly).

SEE ALSO

  share remove       Remove a share
  article list --feed   See articles from followed users

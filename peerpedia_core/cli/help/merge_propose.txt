Propose merging your fork's changes back into the original paper.
After forking a paper, editing it, and improving it, use this to
suggest the original author adopt your changes.

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       → you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       → you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       → or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --title means "the title comes next"
                       --scores means "the scores come next"
                       Some flags take a value (--title "My Paper")
                       Others work alone (--mine, --force, --feed).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.


EXAMPLES
  # Propose a merge (your fork → original paper)
  peerpedia merge propose def67890 --target abc12345
  # → def67890 is your fork (the improved version).
  #   abc12345 is the original paper "Radioactivity".
  #   The original author (Pierre) receives a notification.

  # Propose by matching the title instead of the ID
  peerpedia merge propose "Radioactivity fork" --target "Radioactivity"
  # → PeerPedia finds your fork by the title "Radioactivity fork" and the
  #   original by "Radioactivity". If more than one match, you pick from
  #   a list. Both arguments accept title keywords the same way.

  # What Pierre sees after you propose
  # The original author's notification panel shows:
  #   New proposal from @marie (proposal-1)
  #     Fork:   def67890 "Radioactivity (corrected version)"
  #     Target: abc12345 "Radioactivity"
  # Pierre can review the differences first:
  peerpedia article diff def67890 abc12345
  # → Shows every change between your fork and the original.
  #   If they look good, Pierre accepts with:
  #     peerpedia merge accept proposal-1 --target abc12345

  # What to do if the author is not interested
  peerpedia merge withdraw proposal-1
  # → If the original author says no, or simply does not respond, you can
  #   withdraw your proposal. Your fork stays exactly as it is — all your
  #   changes remain. You can re-propose later if you make more edits.

  # Error: proposing to a paper that is not the source
  peerpedia merge propose def67890 --target xyz99999
  # → Error: xyz99999 is not the parent of fork def67890.
  #   You can only merge a fork back to the paper it was forked from.
  #   Check the parent ID with "peerpedia article show def67890".

WHEN TO USE THIS COMMAND
  • You have forked a paper and made significant improvements.
  • You want the original author to consider adopting your changes.
  • You want to be credited as a contributor to the original paper.

FLAGS
  Each flag is shown in the examples above.  This reference explains it
  in full — come back after you have tried the examples.

  --target <id>        The original paper you want your changes merged
                       into.  This is the paper you forked from, not
                       your fork.  Default: (required, no default).
                       Shown in all examples above.

SEE ALSO
  fork               Fork a paper first
  merge accept       Accept a merge proposal (as the original author)
  merge withdraw     Withdraw your proposal

Follow another researcher.  Their papers will appear in your personal
feed (article list --feed), and you'll be notified of their new work.

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       → you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       → you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       → or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --title means "the title comes next"
                       --scores means "the scores come next"
                       Some flags take a value (--title "My Paper")
                       Others work alone (--mine, --force, --feed).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.


EXAMPLES
  # Follow by @name (easiest — use their display name with @)
  peerpedia follow @marie_curie
  # → Now following Marie Curie

  # Follow by user ID
  peerpedia follow abc12345
  # → Now following Albert Einstein

  # Follow by partial ID
  peerpedia follow abc1
  # → Now following Albert Einstein

  # Follow by alias (if you set one)
  peerpedia follow marie
  # → Now following Marie Curie

WHAT "FOLLOWING" MEANS
  • The user's papers appear in your feed (article list --feed).
  • You get notified when they publish something new.
  • The followed user gets a notification that you followed them.
  • Following is how you build your reading list — it's like
    subscribing to a researcher's work.

Don't know who to follow?  Try:
  peerpedia school          — most-followed users (popular in the community)
  peerpedia account search  — find specific people by name

HOW TO UNFOLLOW
  Use 'peerpedia unfollow @name' to stop following someone.
  Unfollowing is private — they are not notified.

FLAGS
  This command takes one required piece of information: who to follow.
  Provide it as an @name, user ID, or UUID prefix. No flags needed.
  --json      Print output as machine-readable JSON. Default: off.

SEE ALSO
  unfollow           Stop following someone
  article list --feed    See papers from people you follow
  school             Discover popular users to follow
  alias set          Give a followed user a short nickname

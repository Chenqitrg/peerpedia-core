Stop following a user.  Their articles will no longer appear in your
feed, and they will no longer receive notifications about your activity.
This command is safe to run even if you don't currently follow the user.

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       → you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       → you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       → or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --title means "the title comes next"
                       --scores means "the scores come next"
                       Some flags take a value (--title "My Paper")
                       Others work alone (--mine, --force, --feed).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.


EXAMPLES
  # Unfollow by @name
  peerpedia unfollow @einstein
  # → Stopped following Albert Einstein

  # Unfollow by user ID
  peerpedia unfollow abc12345
  # → Stopped following Albert Einstein

  # Unfollow by alias
  peerpedia unfollow albert
  # → Stopped following Albert Einstein

  # Unfollow someone you don't actually follow (safe — no error)
  peerpedia unfollow @stranger
  # → Stopped following Stranger

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER UNFOLLOWING
  • Their papers disappear from your feed (article list --feed).
  • You stop getting notifications about their activity.
  • Unfollowing is private — the user is NOT notified.
  • Their existing papers remain in your local database.
    You can still read them with 'article show' or see them
    with 'article list'.

  You can follow someone again anytime with 'peerpedia follow @name'.

FLAGS
  This command takes one required piece of information: who to unfollow.
  Provide it as an @name, user ID, or UUID prefix. No flags needed.
  --json      Print output as machine-readable JSON. Default: off.

SEE ALSO

  following          See who you follow
  article list --feed   See your current feed

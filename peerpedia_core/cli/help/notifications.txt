View your notifications.  PeerPedia sends you notifications when:
  • Someone reviews your paper
  • An author replies to your review
  • You receive a review invitation
  • Someone proposes a merge to your paper
  • Someone follows you
  • Your paper transitions from sedimentation to published

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       → you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       → you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       → or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --title means "the title comes next"
                       --scores means "the scores come next"
                       Some flags take a value (--title "My Paper")
                       Others work alone (--mine, --force, --feed).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.


EXAMPLES
  # Show unread notifications (default — shows only unread)
  peerpedia notifications
  # → 3 unread notification(s)
  #
  #   abcdef01  2026-06-26 14:30  @pierre reviewed your paper "Radioactivity"  NEW
  #   abcdef02  2026-06-26 13:15  @marie replied to your review on "Quantum Error Correction"  NEW
  #   abcdef03  2026-06-26 12:00  @einstein started following you  NEW

  # Show all notifications, including read ones
  peerpedia notifications --all
  # Shows everything. Read notifications have no "NEW" tag.

  # No notifications yet
  peerpedia notifications
  # → No notifications.

  # What each notification type means
  # NEW  @pierre reviewed your paper "Radioactivity"
  #       Someone submitted a peer review of your paper.
  #       See their scores with: peerpedia review list <article_id>
  #
  # NEW  @marie replied to your review on "Quantum Error Correction"
  #       The author responded to feedback you gave on their paper.
  #
  # NEW  @einstein started following you
  #       Another user is now following your work.
  #       See their profile with: peerpedia account search Einstein

  # Show notifications as JSON (for scripts)
  peerpedia notifications --json
  # → [{"id": "abcdef01", "event": "review_submitted", ...}, ...]

Notifications are local — they appear in your terminal.  There is
no email or push notification.  Check them regularly to stay up
to date with your papers and reviews.

TIPS
  • Notifications with "NEW" are unread. Mark them read with
    "peerpedia notifications read <id>".
  • The notification ID is the short code at the start of each line
    (abcdef01). You need this to mark a notification as read.
  • Notifications are stored locally, so they persist even when
    you're offline. They sync when you connect to a peer server.

FLAGS
  --all     Show all notifications, including ones already marked as
            read. Default: off (shows only unread). Shown in example above.
  --json    Print output as machine-readable JSON. Default: off.
            Shown in example above.

SEE ALSO
  notifications read   Mark a notification as read
  review list          See reviews directly

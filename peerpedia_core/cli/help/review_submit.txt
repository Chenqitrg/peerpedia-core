Submit a peer review of someone else's paper.  Your review includes
numerical scores and a written comment.  Reviews are public and signed
with your key — they become part of the paper's permanent record.

SCORING DIMENSIONS (each 1–5, from poor to exceptional)
  orig     originality    — novelty of the idea or approach
  rigor    rigor          — methodological soundness
  comp     completeness   — coverage of edge cases and limitations
  ped      pedagogy       — clarity of exposition
  imp      impact         — potential significance to the field

EXAMPLES
  # Review a paper in the sedimentation pool
  peerpedia review submit abc12345 \
      --scores "orig=4,rigor=3,comp=4,ped=3,imp=5" \
      --comment "This paper presents a compelling argument for..."

  # Your comment must be at least 200 characters.  Write a thoughtful
  # review that helps the author improve their work.  Explain what's
  # good, what could be better, and why you gave each score.

  # Review by title keyword
  peerpedia review submit "Radioactivity" \
      --scores "orig=5,rigor=4,comp=4,ped=5,imp=5" \
      --comment "An excellent treatment of the subject..."

WHAT MAKES A GOOD REVIEW
  • Be specific — point to sections, arguments, or data.
  • Explain your scores — why did you give a 3 instead of a 4?
  • Suggest improvements, not just criticism.
  • Your review is signed and public — write professionally.

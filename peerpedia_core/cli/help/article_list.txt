Browse, search, and filter papers.  This is your main way to discover
what's on PeerPedia — from your own drafts to published work by others.

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       → you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       → you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       → or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --title means "the title comes next"
                       --scores means "the scores come next"
                       Some flags take a value (--title "My Paper")
                       Others work alone (--mine, --force, --feed).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.


EXAMPLES
  # All public papers
  peerpedia article list

  # Your own papers (including private drafts)
  peerpedia article list --mine

  # Your drafts only
  peerpedia article list --mine --status draft

  # Papers from researchers you follow (your personal reading list)
  peerpedia article list --feed

  # Search by title keyword
  peerpedia article list --search "quantum"

  # Filter by status
  peerpedia article list --status published       # finished papers
  peerpedia article list --status sedimentation   # papers under review

  # Your bookmarked papers
  peerpedia article list --bookmarked

  # Find a specific researcher's papers (requires a peer server)
  peerpedia article list --user abc12345 --server https://peer.example.com

  # No results found?  Try widening your search
  peerpedia article list --search "quantum"
  # → If nothing shows up, try:
  #     - Use fewer words:  --search "quantum"  instead of  --search "quantum entanglement in cold atoms"
  #     - Remove filters:  try without --status, --mine, or --feed
  #     - Check for typos in your search term
  #     - The paper might be in draft (private) — only the author can see it

COMBINING FILTERS
  You can combine multiple filters to narrow results:

  # Your drafts that mention quantum
  peerpedia article list --mine --status draft --search "quantum"

  # Published papers from your feed about machine learning
  peerpedia article list --feed --status published --search "machine learning"

UNDERSTANDING PAPER STATUS
  draft         — private, only you and co-authors can see it
  sedimentation — public review period (3 days, then auto-publishes)
  published     — permanent and citable

FLAGS
  Each flag is shown in the examples above.  This reference explains them
  in full — come back after you have tried the examples.

  --search "keyword"   Filter papers whose title contains the keyword.
                       Case-insensitive.  Matches partial words.
                       Example: --search "quantum" finds "Quantum Error..."

  --status <s>         Show only papers with this status:
                         draft         — your private drafts
                         sedimentation — papers under public review
                         published     — finished, citable papers
                       Example: --status draft

  --feed               Show papers from researchers you follow.  This is
                       your personal reading list — it updates as the
                       people you follow publish new work.

  --mine               Show only your own papers (includes private drafts
                       that no one else can see).

  --bookmarked         Show only papers you have bookmarked.

  --user <id>          Show papers by a specific researcher.  Requires
                       --server to fetch from a peer.
                       The <id> can be a full UUID, first 8 characters,
                       or @name.  Example: --user @marie_curie

  --server <url>       The peer server to query for remote --user lookup.
                       Example: --server https://peer.example.com

Walk the social graph to discover new articles.  Starting from users you
follow, PeerPedia looks at who they follow, fetches their articles, and
continues outward up to a set depth.

Think of it like exploring the "neighborhood" of your academic network.
Each hop outward brings you to people one degree further from your
direct follow list.

HOW TO READ THIS HELP
  The examples below show real commands you can copy and paste.
  Everything you should type is shown — just replace the example values
  with your own.

    <angle brackets>   Replace with your own value.  Do NOT type the brackets.
                       peerpedia article show <id>
                       → you actually type:  peerpedia article show abc12345

    [square brackets]  This part is optional.  You can skip it entirely.
                       peerpedia article list [--mine]
                       → you can type:  peerpedia article list
                       → or:            peerpedia article list --mine

    --double-dashes    A "flag" (option) that changes what the command does.
                       --title means "the title comes next"
                       --scores means "the scores come next"
                       Some flags take a value (--title "My Paper")
                       Others work alone (--mine, --force, --feed).

  The prompt character > or $ in examples means "type this in your terminal."
  Don't type the prompt itself.


EXAMPLES
  # Discover articles one hop away (default depth = 1)
  peerpedia sync discover
  # → Network discovery complete:
  #     Users discovered: 12
  #     Articles discovered: 34
  #     Follows added: 8
  #     Depth reached: 1

  # Go deeper into the network (who your friends' friends follow)
  peerpedia sync discover --depth 3
  # → Network discovery complete:
  #     Users discovered: 156
  #     Articles discovered: 423
  #     Follows added: 89
  #     Depth reached: 3

  # Limit the number of users to traverse
  peerpedia sync discover --depth 2 --max-users 50
  # Stops after discovering 50 users, even if depth 2 would
  # have found more. Good for a quick check.

  # Discover with JSON output
  peerpedia sync discover --json
  # → {"users_discovered": 12, "articles_discovered": 34,
  #     "follows_added": 8, "depth_reached": 1}

WHEN TO USE DISCOVER
  • You just joined and want to find interesting people and papers.
  • You want to go beyond your direct follow list.
  • You're looking for papers in a specific community.

RULES
  • Discovery walks the follow graph on the peer server.
  • For each discovered user, their public articles are pulled
    and merged into your local database.
  • Discovery requires a configured peer server.
  • The deeper you go, the more users and articles you'll find,
    but it also takes longer.

FLAGS
  --depth       How many hops outward to traverse. Default: 1.
                Depth 1 = people you follow. Depth 2 = people they
                follow. Shown in examples above.
  --max-users   Stop after discovering this many users. Default: 100.
                Prevents the command from taking too long on large
                networks. Shown in examples above.
  --server      Which server to discover through. Default:
                PEERPEDIA_SERVER env var.
  --json        Print output as machine-readable JSON. Default: off.
                Shown in example above.
